Introduction

The Northwest Advanced Programming Workship is a coding program that occurs every summer. High school students from all over the Pacific Northwest work together for around three weeks and try to complete the year's goals. By participating in this program, students learn more than what they could in school, such as working in a large group and a product-oriented mindset.

Year Two (2018)

Year Two began with dividing into groups: one working on speed control, one working on steering and staying within lanes, one working on image processing, and one working on GPU optimization. Other groups did things like making sure the car had up-to-date code, looking at ways to get the cost down, and building the website that you are reading right now.
